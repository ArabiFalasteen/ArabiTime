🕌 Palestinian Culture

Context Makes Language Come Alive

From Areej, with love 🇵🇸

🛒 Bargaining — المساومة

In traditional Palestinian markets, prices are negotiable. Bargaining isn't rude — it's expected and even enjoyed as a social ritual.

بكم هاد؟ — How much is this? هاد غالي — This is expensive يسلمو! — Thank you / May you be protected

Start with bkam haad?, then shake your head gently and say haad ghaali. The seller will often lower the price — sometimes several times!

🧆 Nablus — نابلس

The old market of Nablus is famous for:

• كنافة (kanafeh) — the legendary cheese pastry

• صابون النابلسي — Nabulsi olive oil soap, exported since the 10th century

• Spices & za'atar — the fragrance of every alley

Wandering through the souk is a cultural experience — every shop has a story, every shopkeeper wants to offer you tea.

🫒 Bethlehem — بيت لحم

Bethlehem is known for exquisite handcrafts:

• Olive wood carvings — nativity scenes, crosses, intricate boxes

• Mother-of-pearl jewelry

• Handwoven embroidery (tatreez) — the red cross-stitch patterns you'll see everywhere

Shopping here supports local Palestinian artisans and families directly. Every purchase has a story behind it.

☕ Coffee Dialects — القهوة

Arabic coffee has many names across the Arab world:

بدي قهوة / أهوة / كهوة / گهوة

In Palestinian Arabic you might hear qahwa, ahwa, or kahwa — all mean the same thing. Don't worry about the "correct" way, just ask and you'll get something delicious!

يسلمو — yislamu — Thank you (lit. "May you be protected")
🌿 Language and culture are inseparable. When you learn المساومة (bargaining), you're not just learning words — you're stepping into a tradition thousands of years old.
